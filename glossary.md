# Glossary

:::{glossary}

CZ
: Critical Zone. Earth's critical zone is the “heterogeneous, near surface environment in which complex interactions involving rock, soil, water, air, and living organisms regulate the natural habitat and determine the availability of life-sustaining resources” {cite}`national2001basic` The Critical Zone, surface and near-surface environment, sustains nearly all terrestrial life.


ERT
: Electrical Resistivity Tomography. A geophysical method that maps subsurface resistivity using surface electrodes.

EMI
: Electromagnetic Induction. Measures apparent electrical conductivity without direct ground contact.

GPR
: Ground-Penetrating Radar. Uses radar pulses to image subsurface structures via dielectric contrasts.


petrophysics
: The study of the physical and chemical properties of rocks, soils, and their
  contained fluids. In applied geophysics, petrophysics provides the quantitative
  link — known as a **petrophysical transfer function** — between a measurable
  geophysical quantity (e.g. electrical resistivity) and a target physical property
  (e.g. porosity or water saturation). These relationships are empirical,
  lithology-dependent, and require site-specific calibration.

Faraday's law
:  A changing magnetic field induces an electromotive force (EMF) in any nearby
  closed conductor.

Electromotive force (EMF)
:  The voltage generated in a circuit by a changing magnetic flux.

Eddy currents
:  Loops of electrical current induced in a conductor by a changing magnetic
  field. In EMI, they are induced in the ground by the transmitter coil.

Electrical conductivity
:  The ability of a material to conduct electrical current, denoted $\sigma$
  (S/m). The inverse of electrical resistivity.

Magnetic permeability
:  The ability of a material to support the formation of a magnetic field,
  denoted $\mu$ (H/m). Relevant in magnetically susceptible soils.

Electrical permittivity
:  The ability of a material to store electrical energy in an electric field,
  denoted $\varepsilon$ (F/m). Dominates at high frequencies (e.g. GPR).

EMI
:  Electromagnetic Induction. A geophysical method that uses low-frequency
  electromagnetic fields to measure subsurface electrical conductivity.

Apparent electrical conductivity
:  ECa (mS/m). A bulk, depth-averaged conductivity value. It is not the conductivity at a specific depth.

Coil spacing
:  The fixed distance $s$ between the transmitter and receiver coils of an EMI
  instrument. Controls the depth of investigation.

Primary magnetic field
:  The magnetic field $H_p$ generated directly by the transmitter coil.

Secondary field
:  The magnetic field $H_s$ generated by eddy currents in the ground and
  measured at the receiver coil.

Low induction number (LIN)
:  An approximation valid when the coil spacing is much smaller than the skin
  depth. Allows a simple linear relationship between ECa and $H_s/H_p$.

Skin depth
:  The depth $\delta$ at which the EM field amplitude decays to $1/e \approx 37\%$
  of its surface value, given by $\delta = \sqrt{2 / (\omega \mu_0 \sigma)}$.
  Increases with decreasing frequency and decreasing conductivity — it sets the
  maximum depth from which useful signal can be recovered. As a rule of thumb,
  $\delta \approx 503 / \sqrt{f \cdot \sigma}$ (metres): at $f = 10\,\text{kHz}$
  in a typical agricultural soil ($\sigma = 0.05\,\text{S/m}$), $\delta \approx 22\,\text{m}$.

Angular frequency
:  $\omega = 2\pi f$, where $f$ is the operating frequency of the EMI
  instrument (Hz).


Depth of investigation
:  The maximum depth from which an instrument receives a significant signal.

Depth sensitivity
:  The relative contribution of each depth interval to the total ECa measurement.
  Described by the McNeill (1980) sensitivity functions.

HCP
:  Horizontal Coplanar. A coil configuration where both coils lie in a
  horizontal plane, producing a vertical magnetic dipole. Provides deeper
  sensitivity than VCP.

VCP
:  Vertical Coplanar. A coil configuration where both coils stand upright,
  producing a horizontal magnetic dipole. Provides shallower, near-surface
  sensitivity.

Magnetic dipole
:  A simplified model of a coil as a point source of magnetic field. Used in
  analytical sensitivity calculations.
